# Refinement Sprint 001

## Source Feedback

This sprint responds to Lyra's June 30 product review after Momentum and Care landed.

The key direction is not more functionality. It is refinement:

- typography,
- spacing,
- padding,
- alignment,
- micro-copy,
- gentler animation,
- clearer language around Momentum and Care.

## Product Refinements Applied

- Momentum is visually promoted in the six-card grid.
- `Care` becomes `Suggested Care` so it reads more conversationally.
- `+6 expected improvement` becomes Expected Pulse language.
- Browser Health copy now talks about today's experience rather than the browser as a technical object.
- Application copy is calmer and more contextual, especially for Codex and Chrome.

## Deferred Concepts

### Flow Forecast

Flow Forecast is a strong future direction:

> Approximately 4h 18m before System Pulse expects your experience may begin to degrade.

Do not show this in the app until there is an evidence model behind it. A fake forecast would break trust.

### Intent

Intent detection is also deferred.

Example future direction:

- Codex, GitHub, Terminal, and ChatGPT suggest a development session.
- Recommendations can become contextual to the user's current mode of work.

Do not infer user intent in the product until the rules are explicit, explainable, and local-first.

## Guardrail

System Pulse should keep getting simpler as it becomes more useful.

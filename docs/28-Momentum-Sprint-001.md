# Momentum Sprint 001

## Source Feedback

This sprint responds to Lyra's June 30 product review of the second System Pulse build.

The key product direction:

- stop adding system metrics,
- increase human understanding,
- answer whether Vanessa can keep working or whether something is likely to get in the way,
- treat System Pulse as closer to an Apple Health app for the computer than a traditional system monitor.

## Product Decisions Applied

- The Today greeting now addresses Vanessa directly.
- The primary reading flow moves toward: greeting, Heart + score, momentum state, recommendation.
- `Renderer` is removed from the primary interface and folded under Browser Health details.
- `Desktop` becomes Experience.
- The six-card grid is now Memory, Storage, Browser Health, Experience, Applications, and Momentum.
- Application rows now include a Care recommendation generated by PulseCore.

## Guardrails

- Care suggestions do not perform actions.
- System Pulse still does not restart applications.
- System Pulse still does not optimise, clean, delete, or change user data.
- The UI still does not interpret raw metrics.
- PulseCore continues to own scoring, recommendations, Care language, and Momentum language.

## Deferred

Momentum Score and time-to-interruption are promising product ideas, but they are not implemented yet.

They need a real evidence model before appearing in the app. Do not fake a work-duration forecast.
